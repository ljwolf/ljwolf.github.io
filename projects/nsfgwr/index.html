A quick further note on GWR/SVC & the DGPs

Last meeting, I showed you this plot of the SVC bandwidth estimate versus the DGP process’s parameter.

Figure 1: SVC Estimate Versus Process Parameter

So, from Figure 1, we can see that

  • large process parameters1 for the GRF and SAR yield small SVC estimates. So, they agree on what is “global” and what is “local”, since small SVC estimates mean “global” here.
  • the SVC seems insensitive to the Fractal DGP parameter. The “roughness” of a surface doesn’t really affect the bandwidth estimate from an SVC.

In light of these results, we also mentioned converting these into having Moran’s I on the horizontal axis, to be more comparable to Hanchen’s current heatmaps.

Having done this, the plot now shows that the fractal surface comes in line with Hanchen’s results from GWR:

Figure 2: SVC Estimate versus Moran’s I for β

So, on the horizontal axis, we have a surface’s Moran’s I value (estimated from ) and on the vertical axis, we have the SVC bandwidth estimate, as before. This suggests that the SVC bandwidth estimate does track with an estimate of spatial autocorrelation (Moran’s I) for all three DGPs. This matches the weak relationship in Hanchen’s results.

How can the two both be correct, though?

I think this is pretty interesting! It implies that “roughness” is not related to autocorrelation in the surfaces.

So, we can see this directly in the figure below. For the GRF and the SAR, we see that increases in their process parameter result in increases in the Moran’s I for a surface (nearly monotonically increasing!) But, for the fractal process, it falls from perfectly autocorrelated to something around .25:

Figure 3: Moran’s I for β versus Process Parameter

So, the entire “right” side of the Fractal pane above is composed of surfaces with fractal values between 0 and .5. The “swell” of dots between 0 and .5 reflects all fractal parameter realizations from about 0.5 onwards. This is a function of the distinctive structure in these three surfaces. In the Fractal DGP one, you can see that all the high Moran’s I realizations occur with a small fractal roughness parameter, and these generally have large SVC bandwidth estimates.
